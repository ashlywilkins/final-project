Orca Captivity Needs To End

Killer whales, more properly known as orcas, have been kept in captivity since 1961, helpless victims of a blatantly commercial experiment which has seen dozens of wild orcas plucked from their families and forced to live in artificial social groupings which bear scant resemblance to their natural order. Right now there are 42 killer whales being captive world wide. At least 137 killer whales have been taken into captivity since 1961and 124 of th4ese whales are now dead. The medium survival rate for orcas in captivity is approximetly 4 years. For whales that are born in captivity, their lifespan is usually approximately 4.5 years. In total, 156 orca whales have died in captivity in toal, including 28 miscarried or still born calves. There are 44 orcas that have died in Sea World in the United States.
